Myths vs Facts

Breaking down misconceptions about learning disabilities

MYTH

"Learning disabilities are caused by laziness or lack of intelligence"

FACT

Learning disabilities are neurological differences that affect how the brain processes information. They have nothing to do with intelligence or effort.

MYTH

"Children will outgrow their learning disabilities"

FACT

Learning disabilities are lifelong conditions. However, with proper support and strategies, individuals can learn to manage and succeed.

MYTH

"Technology can't help with learning disabilities"

FACT

AI-powered platforms like CognoFlask provide personalized learning experiences that adapt to individual needs and learning styles.

MYTH

"Learning disabilities only affect academic performance"

FACT

Learning disabilities can affect social skills, self-esteem, and daily life activities. Comprehensive support addresses all these areas.

MYTH

"Learning disabilities are rare and affect very few children"

FACT

Learning disabilities affect 15-20% of the population. It's estimated that 1 in 5 children has a learning disability, making it quite common.

MYTH

"Students with learning disabilities should be placed in separate classrooms"

FACT

Inclusive education benefits all students. Children with learning disabilities can thrive in mainstream classrooms with appropriate support and accommodations.

MYTH

"People with learning disabilities can't be successful in life"

FACT

Many successful people have learning disabilities, including entrepreneurs, artists, scientists, and leaders. With proper support, individuals can achieve their goals.
